Get to Know Imam Sherif

Chaplain, counselor, and resident imam — serving Stafford since 2010.

Background

A scholar, chaplain,
and neighbor

Imam Sherif Shehata has served as resident imam of Masjid Aliya in Stafford, Virginia since 2010. In that time he has led thousands of prayers, delivered hundreds of khutbahs, and walked with countless families through their most significant moments — births, marriages, losses, and questions of faith.

"Every door I open is an invitation. Whether you are Muslim or not, whether you have a question or a burden — you are welcome here."

Beyond the masjid, he serves as chaplain to Stafford Fire & Rescue (since 2019) and the Stafford County Sheriff's Office — bringing pastoral care to first responders and those in crisis. He also serves on the Stafford Diversity Advisory Coalition (Vice President) and the Faith Leaders Advisory Committee.

Education & Training

Grounded in two of Egypt's most distinguished institutions of Islamic scholarship.

Cairo, Egypt · Est. 970 AD

Al-Azhar University

Hadith & Islamic Sciences

Al-Azhar is one of the oldest and most respected institutions of Islamic learning in the world — and the foundation of Imam Sherif's scholarly formation. His studies in Hadith provided deep roots in prophetic tradition and the science of narration.

Cairo, Egypt

Ain Shams University

Islamic Law (Sharia)

Ain Shams brought the rigor of legal reasoning to his practice. This training in Islamic jurisprudence underpins his guidance on matters of marriage, family, contracts, and religious practice — offered with care, not just certainty.

Chaplain to Stafford Fire & Rescue

First responders carry weight that most people never see. Imam Sherif serves as chaplain to Stafford Fire & Rescue — providing emotional and spiritual support to firefighters and EMS personnel, facilitating critical incident stress debriefings, and walking alongside families during their hardest hours.

When a call goes wrong, he's the one who helps people process what happened — regardless of their faith tradition.

Chaplain to the Stafford County Sheriff's Office

In partnership with the Stafford County Sheriff's Office, Imam Sherif provides chaplaincy to law enforcement officers, people in detention, and families navigating the justice system.

His presence bridges gaps — offering counsel, dignity, and a steady voice in places where those things are often hard to find.
