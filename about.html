Our story

One imam, many roles

Serving the Stafford community since 2010 — as resident imam, chaplain to fire & rescue, chaplain to the sheriff's office, officiant, counselor, and neighbor.

Imam Sherif keeps his door open to every member of the community — whether you're navigating marriage, raising a family, new to Islam, or simply have something on your heart.

His approach is simple: every person deserves to be heard with care and without judgment. Whether your question is doctrinal or deeply personal, whether you're Muslim or simply curious — the door is open.

That philosophy extends beyond the masjid. As chaplain to Stafford Fire & Rescue and the Stafford County Sheriff's Office, Imam Sherif brings that same quiet presence to the hardest moments first responders and their families face. Confidentiality, compassion, and genuine concern are the constants in every role he holds.

Who he is

Every role, one calling

Imam Sherif wears many hats — each one worn with the same spirit of service.

Resident Imam

Imam of Masjid Aliya since 2010. Leads Fajr through Isha, delivers the Friday khutbah, and guides the community through every milestone of faith life — from birth to marriage to janazah.

Fire & Rescue Chaplain

Chaplain to Stafford Fire & Rescue since 2019. Provides pastoral care to first responders and their families, and brings comfort and presence in the hardest moments on the job and off.

Sheriff's Office Chaplain

Chaplain to the Stafford County Sheriff's Office, supporting law enforcement personnel and those in their care through crisis, loss, and the weight of the work they carry.

Officiant & Counselor

Officiates nikah ceremonies, Islamic divorces, and janazah prayers. Offers confidential marital and family counseling rooted in deep compassion, patience, and discretion.

Community Leader

Vice President of the Stafford Diversity Advisory Coalition and member of the Faith Leaders Advisory Committee — building bridges across faiths, cultures, and generations.

Background & Languages

Trained in Islamic law (Ain Shams University) and Hadith (Al-Azhar University). Speaks English, Arabic, and German. Serves all who seek guidance regardless of background or faith.
